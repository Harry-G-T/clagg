How to play

Clagg (widely known as Oh Hell, and also as Oh Pshaw, Blackout, Bust or Nomination Whist) is a trick-taking game for 3–7 players, best with 4–6. Everyone plays for themselves. The whole point is to win exactly the number of tricks you bid — no more, no fewer.

The deal

A standard 52-card deck is used, ranking high to low: A K Q J 10 9 8 7 6 5 4 3 2. The number of cards dealt changes every hand. A common pattern counts down then back up — for example 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1, then 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 — so the trick count each round equals the number of cards in your hand. After dealing, the next card is turned face up; its suit is trump for that hand and beats all other suits.

Bidding

Starting to the dealer's left and going clockwise, each player predicts how many tricks they will take, from zero up to the number of cards dealt. The dealer bids last.

The hook (dealer rule): a common option forbids the dealer from bidding a number that would make the total of all bids equal the number of tricks available. This guarantees at least one player must miss, adding tension. In this app, the Total bids box turns red when the bids add up to the tricks — that's the hook being broken.

Playing the tricks

The player to the dealer's left leads the first trick. You must follow the suit led if you can; if you have none of that suit, you may play anything, including a trump. A trick is won by the highest trump played, or if no trump was played, the highest card of the suit led. The winner of each trick leads the next. Play until all cards are gone.

Scoring

The most common method: if you take exactly your bid you score a bonus (commonly 10) plus 1 point per trick taken; miss your bid and you score nothing. A successful bid of zero often scores a flat bonus. This app lets you set the bonus, the per-trick value and the miss score yourself in Settings — the default is 10 plus 3 per trick.

The player with the highest score at the end of all the hands wins.

Bonus rounds

Clagg is often spiced up with special rounds. The scorecard's ★ Bonus button lets you add any of these:

Misère — the goal flips: avoid tricks entirely. You lose points for every trick you're forced to win (5 per trick by default).

Bid misère — you actively declare misère. Win no tricks and you're safe (zero); take even one and you pay the penalty per trick.

Bid — trump is auctioned rather than turned up: players bid for it and the highest bidder chooses trump. If bids tie, the earliest bidder wins it. Play then scores as normal.

Blind — you place your bid before looking at your hand.

Half-blind — you may look at your cards, but the trump suit stays hidden until play begins.

Anarchy — the follow-suit rule is dropped: play any card at any time.

Forehead (1-card rounds) — in the first and last hands, when only one card is dealt, you hold it to your forehead: you can see everyone else's card but not your own. You bid on that basis, then all cards flip up for the single trick.

Ascending madness — tricks are worth escalating points: the 1st trick 2, the 2nd 4, the 3rd 6, and so on. Descending madness reverses it: the 1st trick is worth twice the hand size, counting down to 2 for the last. You still bid for the normal 10-point bonus — unless combined with misère, where everyone aims for zero. Because the score depends on which tricks you win, you enter these round scores by hand on the card.
